Analytic Combinatorics,  Modular  Forms,   and  Number  Theory  REU

Description. The USC ACMFNT REU provides undergraduates with an opportunity to experience research in a focused and supportive environment. Students work closely with faculty mentors and peers on current problems in analytic combinatorics and number theory, developing both technical skills and mathematical independence. The program reflects USC’s strengths in analytic and combinatorial aspects of number theory, including topics related to integer partitions, modular forms, and arithmetic sequences. Participants are introduced to modern techniques and ideas that connect classical theory with contemporary methods. The program is part of a broader and active number theory community at USC. The department regularly hosts and participates in regional conferences such as the Palmetto Number Theory Series (PANTS) and SERMON (Southeast Regional Meeting on Numbers), long-standing meetings that bring together researchers from across the Southeast. The core members of the PANTS consortium are the two flagship research universities of South Carolina — the Palmetto State — Clemson University and the University of South Carolina. Through these connections, REU participants gain exposure to the wider mathematical community and the culture of contemporary research. Our goal is to create an environment where students can engage seriously with mathematics, build confidence in their abilities, and take meaningful steps toward future graduate study or research careers.
